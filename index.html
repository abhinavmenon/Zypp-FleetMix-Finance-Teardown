The core question this model asks: Does delivery commission per extra QC trip exceed rider payout per extra trip? If yes, shifting fleet mix to QC improves contribution margin even as total costs rise. If the margin per trip is thin, volume alone won't close the loss gap.

Three findings - sourced
1
Rider expenses (₹355 Cr) scaled 49% alongside delivery revenue growth of 50% - confirming near 1:1 variable cost tracking. Revenue and cost grew together, which is why the loss didn't shrink despite 50% top-line growth. Source: Entrackr, Feb 2026 (RoC filing).
2
Zypp's app states "Higher Daily Earning = Lower Daily Rent" - rental revenue per scooter decreases as delivery volume increases. The two revenue lines move in opposite directions as QC mix rises, offsetting some of the commission gain. Source: Zypp Pilot app, Google Play Store.
3
No per-scooter P&L by delivery type or city exists in any public disclosure. Fleet placement decisions are being made without a contribution margin view by segment. This is the MIS gap - and the question this model is designed to frame. Source: MCA RoC filing analysis.
